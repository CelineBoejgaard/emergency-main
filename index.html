My beach getaway to Portugal didn’t go as planned, but stormy skies helped me discover the heart of the Algarve

I booked a five-day escape to the Algarve in southern Portugal, dreaming of warm sun, turquoise water, and lazy afternoons by the beach. I’d picked a charming boutique hotel in Lagos, with a balcony that overlooked the cliffs and ocean. My plan was simple: sunbathe, swim, repeat.

Unfortunately, the universe had other ideas. The moment I landed, the sky turned gray and the wind began to howl. Locals told me the region was experiencing an unusually long Atlantic storm front — a rarity in late spring. My plans for sun-soaked days by the water vanished overnight.

The beaches were closed due to rough tides, and even the seaside restaurants pulled their tables inside. For a moment, I thought I’d wasted my vacation days on a washout. But as the days went on, I realized the bad weather had given me something even better: the chance to experience the Algarve beyond its beaches.

Once I stopped chasing the sun, I found a deeper connection to the place — and to myself

Determined not to let the rain win, I swapped my swimsuit for a rain jacket and started exploring. I joined a local cooking class in the old town of Lagos, where a grandmotherly chef taught us how to make cataplana, a traditional seafood stew. The kitchen was warm, the laughter was louder than the thunder outside, and it felt like the real Portugal.

I also spent time wandering through cobbled streets, visiting pottery studios, and ducking into cafés filled with locals instead of tourists. One rainy evening, I found a tiny wine bar tucked under an archway, where the owner poured me a glass of vinho verde and talked about how the ocean shaped every part of Algarve life.

On my last day, the clouds finally broke, and I managed to walk along the cliffs of Ponta da Piedade under a glowing sunset. It felt like a reward — but honestly, I didn’t need it. The storm had already made the trip unforgettable.

The bad weather reminded me that sometimes the best travel moments aren’t the ones we plan

Sure, I missed out on beach time, but I gained stories, flavors, and friendships I never would’ve found otherwise. I learned that travel isn’t about perfect conditions — it’s about curiosity and flexibility.

Next time, I might check the forecast a little more carefully. But I also know that if the weather turns, it’s not the end of a vacation — it might just be the beginning of a better one.

How learning to make my own beach drinks helped me bring vacation vibes anywhere

I used to think beach days were only about sunbathing — until I discovered how much more there is to do

For years, my beach trips looked the same: towel, sunscreen, and hours of doing nothing. It was relaxing, sure, but after a while, I started to wonder what I was missing. So on my last trip, I made a promise to try a few new things — and it completely changed how I spend my time by the ocean.

One morning, I joined a group for a sunrise yoga class right on the sand. The sound of waves replaced music, and it was the calmest start to a day I’ve ever had. Later, I rented a paddleboard, something I’d always been nervous to try. I fell in twice, laughed the whole time, and loved it.

Now, I still bring my towel and sunscreen, but I also pack a frisbee, a book, and a curious mindset. Because the best beach days aren’t just about lying still — they’re about making memories in the sand.

I learned how to make the perfect beach drinks — and now I can bring vacation vibes anywhere

Every time I go on a beach trip, I look forward to sipping something cold, colorful, and a little bit tropical. There’s something about a good drink — the clink of ice, the scent of citrus — that instantly makes me feel like I’m on vacation. But I realized I didn’t have to wait for my next getaway to enjoy that feeling.

This summer, instead of spending money on pricey cocktails at beach bars, I decided to learn how to make my own. My goal was simple: create drinks that taste just as good by the ocean as they do in my own backyard.

What started as a small experiment turned into a full-blown hobby. I learned that the secret to a great beach drink isn’t just the alcohol — it’s balance, freshness, and a little creativity.

I started with simple, refreshing ingredients — and found that less is more

The first drink I tried to master was a classic mojito. I muddled mint, lime, and sugar before adding a splash of rum and soda water. It was light, refreshing, and exactly what I wanted under the sun. From there, I started swapping ingredients to mix things up — basil instead of mint, grapefruit instead of lime.

Then came the mocktails. My favorite? Coconut water, pineapple juice, and a squeeze of lime served over crushed ice. It tastes like a tropical breeze and keeps you hydrated, which makes it perfect for beach days.

I even found ways to prep ahead. I’d mix big batches of base ingredients — like fresh juice blends or homemade simple syrup — in glass bottles. That way, I could pour a quick drink wherever I was, whether that was a picnic, a balcony, or a spot in the sand.

The best beach drinks don’t need a beach at all

Once I got the hang of it, I started inviting friends over for “sunset hour.” We’d play beachy music, pour drinks into colorful glasses, and pretend we were on an island. It wasn’t about the setting — it was about the mood.

My go-to recipes are now a mix of fruity, fizzy, and fun. Think: watermelon spritzes, ginger-lime coolers, and frozen mango margaritas. The trick is using real fruit, plenty of ice, and something sparkling for that instant vacation vibe.

I still love ordering a cocktail by the ocean when I travel. But learning how to make my own has been even more satisfying. No matter where I am — a beach, a park, or my kitchen — one sip and I’m right back on holiday.
